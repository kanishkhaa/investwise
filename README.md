# InvestWise – First Investment App for Beginners

## Mission

Build a first investment app for an 18–22-year-old who has never invested before.

This is **not a Zerodha clone** and **not a trading platform**.

The goal is to become the app that determines whether a young person starts their investing journey with confidence or gives up because investing feels too complex, risky, or overwhelming.

---

# Problem Statement

## Who is the User?

### User Persona

**Name:** Rahul
**Age:** 20
**Occupation:** College Student
**Investment Experience:** Never invested before

---

## Key User Problems

### 1. Information Overload

Many beginners do not know where to start and feel overwhelmed by the amount of financial information available.

### 2. Fear of Losing Money

Investing involves real money, which creates anxiety and hesitation.

### 3. Lack of Financial Education

Most users lack foundational knowledge about investing and finance.

### 4. Too Many Choices

The abundance of investment options creates decision paralysis.

### 5. Complex Language

Financial platforms often use terminology designed for experts.

### 6. Low Confidence

Many users think:

> "I'll invest after I learn more."

Unfortunately, that "later" often never comes.

### 7. Emotional Investing

Many investment decisions are influenced by social media, trends, and emotions rather than understanding.

### 8. No Clear Goal

Users often don't know why they are investing in the first place.

---

# Understanding the Problems & Proposed Solutions

## Problem: Users don't know where to start

### Solution

Provide a step-by-step guided journey that clearly explains the investment learning path.

---

## Problem: Too much information upfront

### Solution

Introduce concepts gradually through beginner-friendly modules.

Use gamification:

* Levels
* XP
* Progress tracking
* Badges

This helps users understand where they are in their journey and increases confidence.

---

## Problem: Lack of Financial Education

### Solution

Create a structured learning pathway that teaches:

* Financial basics
* Investment fundamentals
* Risk concepts

Use active learning and quizzes to reinforce knowledge.

---

## Problem: Complex Financial Terminology

### Solution

Simplify investment language and provide easy explanations for financial terms whenever they appear.

---

## Problem: Emotional Investing

### Solution

Encourage informed decision-making by helping users evaluate:

* What they are investing in
* Why they are investing
* Whether they actually understand the investment

---

## Problem: Fear of Losses

### Solution

Provide realistic simulations showing:

* Market fluctuations
* Gains
* Losses

This helps users understand risk before investing real money.

# Investwise

Investwise is a gamified investing literacy platform .It guides users through a structured learning journey, evaluates knowledge via a quiz, helps them visualize risk, and provides an offline practice simulator.

## What’s included

### 1) User onboarding (`/onboarding`)
A short guided questionnaire (text + multiple-choice steps) that collects:
- Name
- Investment goal
- Time horizon
- Risk comfort level
- Monthly capacity
- Experience level


### 3) Learning path (`/learn`)
A module-based curriculum 

**Key features:**
- Modules listed with lock/unlock behavior based on completion order.
- Each module opens in a modal dialog:
  - Shows lesson visual + title + explanation.
  - Progress bar for the current module.
  - “Next lesson” / “Complete module” CTA.
- Completing a module:
  - Marks module as done.
  - Adds XP (+50 per module).
  - Bumps streak.
  - Triggers confetti.
  - Awards badges based on how many modules were completed:
    - **Foundations** (after a few modules)
    - **Graduate** (after all modules)

**Progress UI:**
- Shows total modules complete, streak days, XP, readiness score, and earned badges.


### 4) Knowledge quiz (`/quiz`)
A multi-question knowledge test using `QUIZ` from `src/lib/curriculum.ts`.

**Key features:**
- One question at a time with selectable options.
- After picking an option, the UI reveals correctness and explanation.
- Finishing the quiz updates:
  - **readinessScore** (best score kept)
  - **quizBest** (best raw score)
  - adds XP: `score * 10`
  - bumps streak
- Badge + confetti when the user performs well:
  - **“Quiz Whiz”** awarded when the result is strong (≥ 80%)


### 5) Risk visualization (`/risk`)
Visual risk explainer for three asset categories:
- FD (Fixed Deposit)
- Index Fund
- Single Stock

**Key features:**
- Asset tabs switch the model parameters (mean + volatility).
- Sliders control:
  - amount invested
  - time horizon (years)
- Shows:
  - A rotating “Risk Meter” (low/medium/high)
  - Best / likely / worst outcome panels
  - A live-updating area chart (historical-style projection)


### 6) Compare outcomes (`/compare`)
Side-by-side projection for the same initial amount over a chosen period.

**Key features:**
- Sliders for amount and years.
- Line chart shows:
  - FD likely projection
  - Index and Stock likely + best + worst bands
- Summary cards display final values and % differences.


### 7) Practice Investment Sandbox (`/simulate`)
A simulated environment allows users to experience market fluctuations without risking real money.

**Key features:**
- Select a mock instrument (tabs):
  - RELIANCE, TCS, INFY, HDFCBANK, NIFTYBEES
- Live chart:
  - Prices are generated by a local random process.
  - Chart updates every ~1.8s.
- Trade panel:
  - Enter quantity
  - Buy:
    - checks cash availability
    - updates positions and average buy price
    - awards badge **“Trader”**
  - Sell:
    - checks holdings
    - updates quantity (removes position if qty becomes 0)
    - shows success/warning toast depending on P&L
- Portfolio header:
  - total equity
  - cash
  - holdings value
  - total P&L vs starting cash
- Reset button restores the practice portfolio:
  - cash = Can be edited by the user
  - empty positions
